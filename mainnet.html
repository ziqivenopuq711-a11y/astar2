Overview

Mainnet Overview

Sonic mainnet is the production-grade, decentralized blockchain platform that serves as the foundation for the next generation of DeFi applications.

Sonic mainnet (Chain ID: 146) represents the production culmination of years of research and development that began with Fantom Opera. While Fantom established itself as a high-performance alternative to Ethereum, Sonic takes performance to an entirely new level — delivering 400,000 TPS capability alongside sub-second deterministic finality, all while maintaining 100% backward compatibility with the Ethereum Virtual Machine and its entire ecosystem of tools and protocols.

The evolution from Fantom to Sonic was not a simple upgrade — it involved a ground-up redesign of the execution engine (SonicVM), the state storage layer (SonicDB), and the fee model (FeeM). The result is a blockchain that feels familiar to Ethereum developers — the same Solidity contracts, the same ABI encoding, the same RPC API — but operates at a speed and cost that makes entirely new categories of applications feasible. Games requiring dozens of on-chain transactions per user session, real-time prediction markets updating on every price tick, and high-frequency trading protocols with sub-second settlement are all practical on Sonic mainnet in ways they are not on slower, more expensive chains.

🔗
Evolved from Fantom Opera
Sonic preserves full state and account continuity from Fantom Opera. Projects that operated on Fantom can migrate their contracts and user accounts to Sonic mainnet without data loss. The migration path is designed to be seamless, with Sonic's higher performance immediately available after migration.
💎
Production-Grade Infrastructure
Sonic mainnet is backed by a distributed set of professional validators running enterprise-grade hardware. The Lachesis aBFT consensus ensures liveness and safety even under adverse network conditions. Validator incentives are aligned with network health through staking rewards and FeeM participation.

Deployment Guide

Deploying to Mainnet

A production deployment checklist covering security, configuration, gas optimization, and FeeM registration.

  1. Complete Blaze testnet validation

    Ensure all contracts are fully tested on Blaze testnet (Chain ID 64165). Verify contracts on the testnet explorer and confirm all integration tests pass against the live testnet, not just local simulations.

  2. Security audit

    For any contract holding or managing user funds, complete a professional security audit before mainnet deployment. Common firms experienced with Sonic EVM contracts include Certik, Trail of Bits, and Halborn. Address all high and critical findings before deployment.

  3. Configure mainnet RPC

    Update your deployment scripts to target https://rpc.soniclabs.com with Chain ID 146. Use a separate mainnet private key stored in a hardware wallet or secure key management system — never reuse testnet keys on mainnet.

  4. Gas optimization with SonicVM

    Benchmark your contract's gas usage on Blaze. SonicVM's super instructions optimize certain opcode patterns automatically, so contracts with tight loops or frequent SLOAD/SSTORE operations may use significantly less gas than on Ethereum. Adjust gas limits accordingly.

  5. Deploy and verify

    Deploy using forge script --rpc-url sonic --broadcast --verify or Hardhat's deploy script. Verify source code on sonicscan.org immediately after deployment so users can inspect your contracts.

  6. Register for FeeM

    After deployment, register your contract addresses for Fee Monetization (FeeM) to earn up to 90% of the gas fees your users pay. Registration is on-chain, permissionless, and takes effect immediately. See the FeeM documentation for details.

Ecosystem

DeFi Ecosystem on Sonic

A rapidly growing ecosystem of DeFi protocols, DEXs, lending platforms, and infrastructure projects building on Sonic's high-performance foundation.

The Sonic mainnet ecosystem has seen rapid growth since launch, driven by the network's technical advantages and the financial incentives created by FeeM. Decentralized exchanges were among the first to launch on Sonic, attracted by the network's ability to process high-frequency trading activity without the congestion and gas spikes that plague Ethereum-based DEXs during volatile market conditions. Sub-second finality means DEX users experience instant trade confirmations rather than the multi-second waits common on other chains.

Lending and borrowing protocols find Sonic's throughput particularly valuable. Liquidation mechanisms — which must execute rapidly when collateral values fall below threshold — can function reliably at Sonic's speeds, reducing systemic risk and enabling more capital-efficient collateral ratios. Liquidators competing to capture liquidation rewards can submit transactions knowing they will be processed in under a second, making the liquidation market more competitive and fair.

🔄
DEXs & AMMs
Decentralized exchanges on Sonic offer users near-instant swap confirmations with negligible gas fees. Sonic's throughput eliminates the queue-based congestion that causes price slippage and failed transactions during high market activity.
🏦
Lending Protocols
Money markets and lending platforms leverage Sonic's speed for reliable liquidations and interest rate updates. Borrowers benefit from responsive collateral management, while lenders enjoy higher protocol safety margins at competitive APYs.
📈
Yield Aggregators
Yield optimization strategies that auto-compound positions or rebalance between pools are economically viable on Sonic due to near-zero gas costs. Complex multi-step strategies that would be cost-prohibitive on Ethereum become profitable on Sonic.

The Sonic Innovator Fund, with its 200 million S token allocation, actively supports new protocols joining the ecosystem. Grants are available for projects that bring novel functionality to Sonic, whether that's a new financial primitive, developer tooling that benefits the entire ecosystem, or applications that drive significant new user adoption. The Fund evaluates applications on technical merit, team quality, and potential ecosystem impact — and actively recruits strong teams to build on Sonic.

💡 FeeM Impact on DeFi

FeeM creates a unique economic dynamic in the Sonic DeFi ecosystem: protocols earn revenue directly from on-chain activity without needing to extract value from users through protocol fees or token inflation. This enables protocols to offer more competitive rates than on other chains while remaining financially sustainable — a fundamental improvement to Web3 protocol economics.
